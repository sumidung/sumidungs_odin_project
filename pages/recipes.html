How to make your own bread at home

Disclaimer: I am no expert at making sourdough this is the recipe from my first ever selfmade sourdough bread. But since the Odin Project asks you to make a recipe page, here we go!

First you need to make a sourdough starter

  1. Start with a clean and clear jar
  2. Put in 30g of lukewarm water and 30g of flour (I used rye since it is supposed to be easier to handle)
  3. Now mix very well
  4. Cover the jar but not airtight
  5. Let that sit for 24 hours at a warm place
  6. Now for the next 4-5 days take away 30g from the sourdough and feed it again with 30g of flour and 30g of water. By day 3 your sourdough should be starting to build bubbles and double in size. Don't worry if it smells weird! It is normal for the sourdough starter to smell weird alcoholic/sour in the beginning. Usually the smell should settle to a nice yeasty dough smell by day 5. But: if it smells like nail polish remover or you can see mold or a rose/orange liquid on top, throw it away and start again!

Alright now we can start making bread. My first one was a 50/50 wheat/rye flour sourdough bread

  1. Take 30g of your sourdough starter and mix it with 100g of water and 100g flour
  2. Let that sit for about 12 hours. sometimes it is less sometimes it is more. When it has doubled in size you can use it
  3. Now take mix all these ingredients together:
    • the sourdough starter
    • 250g rye flour
    • 250g wheat flour
    • 350g water
    • 12g salt
    It will be very sticky because of the rye!

  4. Let the dough sit for the bulk fermantation. I did it overnight in the fridge as this is supposed to make your bread more aromatic but if you are impatient 1 hour in the warmth should do it as well
  5. Shape your bread and put it in your baking dish of choice
  6. Preheat your oven to 250°C and add a bowl of water so ther is steam
  7. Now bake your bread for 10 minutes with the steam
  8. Remove the water bowl and lower the temperatur to 200°C
  9. Finish baking your bread 45-50 minutes
  10. Let it cool down for 12-18 Hours
  11. Last but not least: Enjoy!!!
